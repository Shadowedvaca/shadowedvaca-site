What Is Meandering Muck?

Meandering Muck is a tilt-controlled maze game. You navigate a slime through procedurally generated mazes using your phone's motion controls. Think Cut the Rope or Threes: a simple puzzle premise, done well.

There are no enemies to fight. No health bars. No death. The slimes are pacifists‚ÄîNPCs are absorbed, not killed. The only opponent is the maze itself, and optionally, your own best time.

The Story

The game began at a game jam ten years ago. The theme was "loops," and I built a recursive maze generation algorithm with my son and then-wife using Python and a game engine from a programming book. The algorithm worked. The game didn't ship. But the idea stuck.

Over the next decade, I refined the maze generation system‚Äîtweaking the recursion, balancing difficulty, perfecting the feel of a maze that's challenging but fair. It became a side project I kept returning to, always knowing there was something there but never finding the right form for it.

In September 2024, I was laid off from my job. A few months later, while working on a PC version of the game, the idea finally clicked: what if the mazes used phone tilt controls? The combination of procedurally generated mazes and motion-based navigation turned a puzzle game into something physical and immediate. Meandering Muck found its form.

I'm not an artist or sound designer, so I looked to people around me. Oreo, a friend of my daughter, created the game's visual identity. Skate, a friend from my World of Warcraft guild, designed the audio. The game became a collaboration between people who wanted to make something together.
